Tagliatelle alla Boscaiola

DESCRIPTION

Tagliatelle alla boscaiola: all the rustic and natural taste in a timeless recipe. We reveal all the secrets to prepare this classic autumn main course that will delight your palate and that of your guests. Let's start with preparing the tagliatelle: you can use store-bought ones, but we can guarantee that making them at home will yield a different result, thanks to the homemade porosity of the pasta sheet!

The pasta alla boscaiola is a dish that combines simple and genuine ingredients to create a burst of flavor in every bite. Porcini mushrooms and sausage make our recipe so delicious, and the version we present to you today is enriched with peeled tomatoes, which allow you to achieve this fantastic sauce. Plenty of Parmesan and you're all set, the tagliatelle alla boscaiola are ready... perfect for many occasions!

INGREDIENTS:

HOMEMADE TAGLIATELLE

  • Type 00 Flour 200g

  • Eggs 2

  • Extra virgin olive oil to taste

  • Fine salt a pinch

FOR THE SEASONING

  • Porcini mushrooms 300 g

  • Sausage 250 g

  • Shallot small

  • White wine half glass

  • Peeled tomatoes 300g

  • Parmigiano Reggiano PDO 40 g

  • Fine salt to taste

  • Black pepper to taste

  • Extra virgin olive oil to taste

Steps

    START WITH THE FRESH PASTA
  1. Pour the flour onto a pastry board and create the classic fountain shape;
    place the eggs in the center.

  2. Add a drizzle of oil.

  3. Add a pinch of salt.

  4. Initially work by beating with a fork.

  5. Then help yourself with a dough scraper.

  6. Knead until you have a uniform mixture

  7. Wrap the dough in a plastic wrap and let it rest for 30 minutes in a cool place.

    8. TAKE CARE OF THE PORCINIS
  8. Remove the larger soil residues with a knife, then clean them with a damp cloth.

  9. Separate the caps from the stems.

  10. Cut the mushrooms into cubes about 1-2 inches

    11. MOVE ON TO THE SAUSAGE
  11. Remove the outer casing.

  12. Crumble it coarsely by hand to form balls about an inch in size.

  13. In a large pan, pour some oil and the chopped shallot.

  14. Let it fry gently, then add the sausage and mushrooms.

  15. Stir well, brown them thoroughly, and deglaze with white wine.

  16. Once the alcohol has evaporated, pour in the crushed peeled tomatoes with a fork or by hand.

  17. Stir well and cover with a lid; let it cook for about 25 minutes

  18. Take the fresh pasta again as soon as the resting time is over.
    Take a piece and sprinkle it with flour.

  19. Use a pasta machine to thin it out;
    pass it several times through the rollers until you get a thickness of about 1/8 inch.

  20. You can fold the pasta on itself as you go to obtain a homogeneous sheet.
    Once you have the sheet, cut out rectangles of pasta.

  21. At this point, roll the pasta rectangle first from one side and then from the other without tightening.

  22. Cut the tagliatelle with a knife to a thickness of about 1/4 inch.

  23. Then wrap them around your hand to form a nest and continue with the others.

  24. At this point, the sauce will be ready.

  25. Cook the tagliatelle in plenty of salted water.

  26. Once cooked after a few minutes, drain them and transfer them directly into the sauce.

  27. Add plenty of chopped parsley.

  28. Saut√© everything well, adding cooking water if needed.

  29. Plate, finish with grated Parmesan.
